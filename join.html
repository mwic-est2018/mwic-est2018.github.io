WHAT TECH CONNECT DOES:

We are an organization that supports women in the tech industry. We provide an environment where we share experiences and learning resources. We hold workshops and coding camps, and we have multiple speakers come to speak to the group.

HOW TO JOIN:

If you love computing and would like to support women in tech, then MWIC is the organization for you! If you are interested in joining or would like to learn more about us, email us at umumwic@mountunion.onmicrosoft.com

WHEN WE MEET:

We meet every Wednesday at 4:15 pm in KHIC 057.

KEEP UP WITH US ON TWITTER:

Find us on Twitter @umuTechConnect where you'll find information about meetings and more!

Supporting the participation of women in computing and building a strong and inclusive community.

WOMEN IN TECH SPOTLIGHT:

ENIAC Programmers: Kay McNulty, Betty Jennings, Betty Snyder, Marlyn Meltzer, Fran Bilas, and Ruth Lichterman

Kay McNulty, Betty Jennings, Betty Snyder, Marlyn Meltzer, Fran Bilas, and Ruth Lichterman were the first programmers of the ENIAC. They were not, as computer scientist and historian Kathryn Kleiman was once told, "refrigerator ladies", i.e., models posing in front of the machine for press photography. Nevertheless, some of the women did not receive recognition for their work on the ENIAC in their lifetimes [wikipedia].
